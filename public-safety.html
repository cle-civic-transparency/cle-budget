Where does the money come from?

Where does the money go?

The chart below shows how money goes from income taxes to policing through the General Fund.

The first column shows all the sources of money for the General Fund. We can see 66% comes from income taxes.

The middle column shows that all money that gets spent on policing goes through The Department of Public Safety. This department makes up 56.26% of the General Fund budget. In comparison, the next largest department (not shown) is the Department of Public Works and it only takes up 12.13%.

The last column shows where the money from Department of Public Safety goes. Department of Public Safety has many divisions with many purposes, but the largest is the Division of Police.

Here are some interesting numbers from the Division of Police budget not captured above:

  • ~$20M is spent each year on hospilizations for officers. That's 10% of the Division of Police budget and twice the size of the Department of Health Budget.
  • The city spends ~200,000 each year on ammunition, a much smaller amount than we would have guessed.
  • The largest expense for the Division of Police department overall are salaries of officers. In 2019, there were 1,336 officers. Each of these officers has a salary of ~$65k.

How has the Cleveland police budget changed over time?

The summer of 2020, protests all around America were happening asking to “Defund the Police.” The topic became a national focus, but still has many open questions. What does it mean to defund the police?

The graph below shows the size of the Department of Public Safety Budget since 2002.

2004: After years of budget deficits, Cleveland city government was finally forced to make budget cuts to the police department. From 2004 to 2015,the Police budget gradually decreased with 2015 marking a 19.2% decrease from 2004.

2012: Timothy Russel and Malissa were shot by the police 137 times.

2014: Tamir Rice, an unarmed 12-year-old with a toy gun, was fatally shot by the Cleveland police at close range in the Cudell Commons park, less than a second after the police arrived.

2016: After several high profile shootings, the Federal Department of Justice issued a consent decree that increased the Department of Safety budget by ~$7.7 M each year.

Budget cuts did not always mean less employees.

2004: Even though 2004 faced huge budget cuts, the number of Division of Police employees increased by 38.

2006: More budget cuts, but 55 employees were added.

2017: Even the Federal Department of Justice's consent decree included an employee increase. Between 2016 and 2017, the Division of Police increased by almost 100 employees.

How have other safety budgets fared in this same period?

  • Fire has experienced a 23.4% decrease in full time staff since 2002, equating to a loss of 237 full time employees. The budget, adjusted for inflation, has experienced a 17.9% decrease over the same period.
  • EMS has experienced a 17.3% increase in full time staff since 2002, equating to a gain of 49 full time staff. The budget, adjusted for inflation, has experienced a 31.2% increase over the same period.

What happens to the Division of Police Budget?

A 15% decrease to the Division of Police means a cut of ~$31M. That's 3x the current Department of Health Budget.

The Division of Police could meet those budget cuts by:

  • Cuting ~467 police officers. That would still leave 869 polices officers in the city.
  • Cut the hospitalization cost in half and cut ~318 police officers. That would still leave over 1,000 officers in the city.
