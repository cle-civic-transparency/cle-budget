Understanding the
Cleveland City Budget

Budgets are moral documents. They reflect what – and who – a city deems worthy of investment.

This is a civic accountability project by a small group of citizens of Cleveland who want to use the budget to hold our politicians accountable to their past promises.

There were 4 steps to accomplish our goal:

  1. Dive into the Cleveland Budget to understand how the city spends money.
  2. Compare the budget to existing promises and visions put forth by civic groups in Cleveland.
  3. Post the results online to promote transparency to all Clevelanders.
  4. Work with activists and civic groups to use the results to promote concrete policy or budget changes.

We spent the past few months diving deep into several categories of city spending: Public Safety (Policing), the Judicial System, Utilities and Infrastructure.

Our initial investigation does not cover topics such as education because its funding mechanisms is extremely complex and goes beyond just the Cleveland city budget. For example, the Cleveland Metropolitan School District has it's own annual budget books with revenue exceeding $1 billion.
