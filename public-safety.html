Where does the money come from?

Funding for public safety comes from the General Fund. This fund covers expenses for what we typically think a city does – the mayor, city council, police and more.

Specifically, the General Fund covers expenses for 11 departments which cover the following areas:

Where does the money go?

The chart below shows how money goes from income taxes to policing through the General Fund. The numbers are for the 2019 unaudited budget.[1] [2]

The first column shows all the sources of money for the General Fund. We can see 66% comes from income taxes.

The middle column shows that all money that gets spent on policing goes through The Department of Public Safety. This department makes up 56.26% of the General Fund budget. In comparison, the next largest department (not shown) is the Department of Public Works and it only takes up 12.13%.

The last column shows where the money from Department of Public Safety goes. Department of Public Safety has many divisions with many purposes, but the largest is the Division of Police.

Divions: Other includes divisions for Fire, Emergency Medical Service (EMS) and Animal Care and Control. Note that the Community Police Review Board, Civialian Police Review Board and Police Inspector General are newer departments that were created from the 2016 Consent Decree. [3]

Here are some interesting numbers from the Division of Police budget not captured above:

  • ~$20M is spent each year on hospilizations for officers. That's 10% of the Division of Police budget and twice the size of the Department of Health Budget.
    • [4]
  • The city spends ~200,000 each year on ammunition, a much smaller amount than we would have guessed.
    • [5]
  • The largest expense for the Division of Police department overall are salaries of officers. In 2019, there were 1,336 officers. Each of these officers has a salary of ~$65k.
    • [6]

How has the budget changed over time?

The summer of 2020, protests all around America were happening asking to “Defund the Police.” The topic became a national focus, but still has many open questions. What does it mean to defund the police?

The graph below shows the size of the Department of Public Safety Budget since 2002.

2004: After years of budget deficits, Cleveland city government was finally forced to make budget cuts to the police department. From 2004 to 2015,the Police budget gradually decreased with 2015 marking a 19.2% decrease from 2004. [7]

2012: Timothy Russel and Malissa were shot by the police 137 times. [8]

2014: Tamir Rice, an unarmed 12-year-old with a toy gun, was fatally shot by the Cleveland police at close range in the Cudell Commons park, less than a second after the police arrived. [9]

2016: After several high profile shootings, the Federal Department of Justice issued a consent decree that increased the Department of Safety budget by ~$7.7 M each year. [10]

Budget cuts did not always mean less employees.

2004: Even though 2004 faced huge budget cuts, the number of Division of Police employees increased by 38.

2006: More budget cuts, but 55 employees were added.

2017: Even the Federal Department of Justice's consent decree included an employee increase. Between 2016 and 2017, the Division of Police increased by almost 100 employees. [11]

What else are people calling for?

Public Safety is more than just policing and there are other calls for support to EMS in the City of Cleveland. Here are some examples:

  • Mental Health Support for EMS: Asked for by the EMS union
  • PTSD Coverage and backpay: Our emergency service first responders have seen some success in winning back pay and PTSD coverage
  • More funding for EMS: the EMS budget increased by 32% from 2011 to 2019, but the call volume during this period increased by 37%
  • Additional EMS staff: Even though the budget allows for a maximum number of employees, difficulties in hiring and training mean that services such as EMS may still be short-staffed, causing the considerable increase in emergency calls to be even more taxing [14]
