Where does the money go?

The first column shows all the sources of money for the General Fund. We can see 66% comes from income taxes.

The middle column shows that all money that gets spent on judicial services to Cleveland Municipal Court with 3 divisions.

  • Judge's Division: The court conducts preliminary hearings and sets bonds in felony cases committed in the City of Cleveland. In civil matters, the Small Claims Division of the Court can determine actions where the amount in controversy does not exceed $6,000.00. The General Division of the Court can determine civil cases where the amount in controversy does not exceed $15,000.00. These civil cases can include, but are not limited to, actions for the recovery of property, injunctions, contracts, personal injury, collection proceedings, and transferred judgments.
  • Clerk's Division: To provide a reliable supply of high quality water and customer services to promote public health and safety, economy, and quality of life of Greater Cleveland.
  • Housin Division: To provide a reliable supply of high quality water and customer services to promote public health and safety, economy, and quality of life of Greater Cleveland.
